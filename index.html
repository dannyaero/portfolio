Formula SAE —
Where I Do Most of My Work

I work across three subteams: Aerodynamics, Chassis, and Ergonomics. Below is a breakdown of what I've actually built, designed, and tested — click a tab to explore.

Aerodynamics

Designed structural and mounting components for the rear wing package — work that has to survive real aero loads at speed while fitting tight packaging constraints.

  • Designed rear wing mounts — structural interface between wing and chassis
  • Designed omega inserts for rear wing airfoils — internal reinforcement geometry for the aero profile
  • Produced engineering drawings for aerodynamic jigs and jig table setups used in fabrication
  • Manufactured aero jig components including parts with countersunk screw hole features

Chassis

Deep involvement in both design and full fabrication process — from jig table setup to weld certification. Also conducting active suspension and impact attenuation testing.

  • Designed the jack bar for chassis lifting and accumulator loading operations
  • Validating roll rates via TR test with suspension springs installed
  • Developing impact attenuator to meet FSAE peak deceleration limits — modeling energy absorption, tuning decel profile, solving for spring constant
  • Completed full chassis fabrication training: jig table setup, alignment, TIG welding, accumulator loading, TR test
  • Pursuing TIG weld certification through team's formal program
  • Produced ARB jig drawings and jig table setups for fabrication

Ergonomics

Driver interface work — designing components that directly affect driver comfort and vehicle control. Also running quantitative testing on steering system behavior.

  • Designed heel rest with full engineering drawings — optimized for driver comfort and pedal geometry
  • Testing steering effort by measuring applied force in pounds at the wheel
  • Quantifying compliance and bending effects on actual vs. calculated steering effort
  • Analyzing impact of rod end removal in secondary steering support on system behavior
